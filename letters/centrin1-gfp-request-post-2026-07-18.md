Does anyone have a **Centrin1-GFP stable cell line** (RPE1-hTERT or HeLa) they would be willing to share?

We are building an open-source robotic microscope for long-term live-cell tracking of centrosome maturation state across divisions. Before committing to the full build, we need to verify that centrioles expressing Centrin1-GFP are detectable at very low LED power (1-5% of max) on a 40×/0.95 dry objective. This is a quick pilot — one afternoon of imaging.

We have budget for a commercial line if needed, but if someone has a line sitting in their freezer, sharing it would save us ~$1,650 and a month of lentivirus work.

**What we need:**
- Any adherent cell line stably expressing Centrin1-GFP (RPE1, HeLa, U2OS — anything with centrioles)
- 1 vial of frozen stock, or even a live dish shipped
- No restrictions — our project is fully open source (GPLv3/CC-BY-SA)

**What we offer in return:**
- Acknowledgement in our paper and repo
- OpenFlexure incubator adaptation data (72h stability, drift, ASA/PETG performance)
- Co-authorship if the pilot data contributes directly to the publication

**What we are building (ARGUS-LP_OS):**
An autonomous microscope inside a CO₂ incubator that tracks which daughter cell inherits the mature mother centrosome across multiple divisions in human neural progenitors. The platform is based on OpenFlexure v6.1.5 and is fully open source.

GitHub: https://github.com/Georgia-Longevity-Alliance/ARGUS-LP
